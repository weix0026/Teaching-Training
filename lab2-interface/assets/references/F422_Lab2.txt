    Lab Handout: Managing a Ponderosa Pine Forest with Linear
                         Programming



1     Introduction
This lab involves managing a ponderosa pine forest using linear programming (LP) to maximize recreation
while meeting timber and sediment constraints. The forest consists of 1,600 ha of high-site land and
2,400 ha of low-site land.


2     Problem Statement
The forest can be managed as a multiple-use area (recreation and timber) or wilderness (recreation only).
Expected outputs per hectare are provided. The goal is to:
    • Maximize recreation (visitor-days per year).
    • Produce at least 1,400 m3/year of timber.
    • Keep sediment below 200 m3/year.


3     LP Formulation
Decision variables:
    • Xhw : Hectares of high-site land as wilderness.

    • Xhm : Hectares of high-site land for multiple use.
    • Xlw : Hectares of low-site land as wilderness.
    • Xlm : Hectares of low-site land for multiple use.
    Objective function:
                             Max Z = Xhw + 0.25Xhm + 0.6Xlw + 0.15Xlm
    Constraints:

                                              Xhw + Xhm ≤ 1600      (high-site land limit)
                                               Xlw + Xlm ≤ 2400     (low-site land limit)
                0.06Xhw + 0.12Xhm + 0.03Xlw + 0.06Xlm ≤ 200       (sediment limit)
                                         3.5Xhm + 1.2Xlm ≥ 1400     (timber requirement)
                                      Xhw , Xhm , Xlw , Xlm ≥ 0


4     Setting Up the Excel Spreadsheet and Solver
Please use the attached Excel template to set up the model.

5     Using Solver
    1. Fill formulas in cells F 6 : F 9 to track the results from management. This is we called Left Hand
       Side.
    2. Enable the Solver add-in via Excel options.
    3. Open Solver, set target cell F11 to Max, by changing cells B3:E3.

    4. Add constraints: F 6 : F 8 ≤ H6 : H8, F 9 ≥ H9, and B3 : E3 ≥ 0.
    5. Select Simplex LP method, solve, and keep the solution.
